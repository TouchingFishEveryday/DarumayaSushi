Alan Mathison Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.

Alan Mathison Turing OBE FRS (/ˈtjʊərɪŋ/; 23 June 1912 – 7 June 1954)

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1912BornAlan Mathison Turing was born on June 23, 1912 in Maida Vale, London.
  • 1926Early Signs of IntelligenceAt the age of 13, Alan Turing first showed signs of intelligence and potential which was noticed at the Sherborne School.
  • 1931Getting Serious About TechnologyAlan Turing studied from 1931 to 1934 at King's College, in Cambridge. He was first in his class and was an honor student in the field of mathematics.
  • 1936Studied at Princeton UniversityTuring studied mathematics and cryptology while also building three of four stages of an electro-mechanical binary multiplier at Princeton University.
  • 1939Hut 8Turing helped to solve the essential part of the German naval indicator system and also came up with the idea of sequential analysis.
  • 1940BombeTuring was responsible for creating the Bombe which was a cryptanalytic machine used to decrypt the Enigma-enciphered messages being sent from the enemies.
  • 1942Travel to USTuring traveled to the United States to work with the US naval cryptanalysts to create their version of the Bombe and an Enigma in Washington.
  • 1946Presented PaperHe presented his paper in February of 1946 where he had presented the first detailed design of a stored program computer setup.
  • 1947ACETuring lived in Hampton, London where worked on the design of the ACE (Automatic Computing Engine).
  • 1948LU decomposition methodTuring created the LU decomposition method that was used and is still used today to solve matrix equations.
  • 1948Software for stored-program computersTuring was appointed a position in the mathematics department at the University of Manchester where he worked on software for one of the earliest stored-program computers.
  • 1950Turing TestThe turing test was created in 1950. The turing test was created to compare the intelligence of a machine/computer to the intelligence of a human brain.
  • 1952Book PublishedTuring's book "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" was published in 1952 which was all about his interest in the biology of organisms.
  • 1952IndecencyTuring was caught in a relationship with an unemployed 19 year old man. He was convicted for indecency because homosexual acts were considered criminal offences in the United Kingdom at the time. This is what lead to his downhill spiral right before his suicide.
  • 1954DeathTuring committed suicide in his house where his housekeeper found him dead.
